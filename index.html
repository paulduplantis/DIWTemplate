Quote from a medical professional on Depression.

Maybe you have a family member who is homeless. Maybe a friend. Maybe a Coworker. Of course you have passed someone on the street who is homeless. What is your first thought? That they chose this way of life? That they want to be there? That they made bad decisions? That the system put them there?

Homelessness is an incredibly complicated cycle of cause and effect whether a situation or illness brought them there or their own free will did. Add onto this the stress created by the rising costs of housing and food in relation to wages and one can see where even a healthy mind could be strained by societal realities. I think it might be fair to say a healthier mental state would be optimal to face such challenges. As these challenges are met collectively, it could be argued society would benefit. It is not to say advocacy for housing access and prices and wage growth is not important. Not in the least. But there is no denying that Mental Health plays a role in the Homelessness crisis where increasing awareness, understanding, compassion and action at the community level could play an important part in taclking the problem.

Depression, Mania, Obsessiveness, Anxiety, and PTSD (Link to our pages here) to name a few are a contributing factor.
